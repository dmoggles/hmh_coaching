Shapes & Movements

Bob-Tails · HMH · 7-a-Side · 2–3–1

Good Shapes

Do this
DM holds, wide mids push
DM stays central between the lines. Both wide mids can push high because she covers the space behind them if the ball turns over.
Wide mid on ball — far side arrives late
Left wide mid has the ball. Right wide mid makes a late run to the far post. DM sits — so if the ball is lost, there's cover in midfield.
CBs — one presses, one covers
Left CB steps up to press the striker. Right CB tucks in centrally and holds — covering the space in behind. Never both step at once.
GK plays wide — not through the middle
Opposition is pressing centrally. GK plays wide to the free CB rather than through the danger zone in the middle.

Dangerous Shapes

Avoid this
Midfield empty on turnover
All three midfielders are forward. The opposition wins the ball and has a clear run at two exposed CBs with no cover.
Both CBs step up — space in behind
Both CBs step up together. The striker runs in behind into the empty space — only the GK to beat.
GK passes through the middle
GK plays through the middle under pressure. Opposition intercepts centrally and is immediately through on goal.
Wide mid doesn't track back — 2v1 on the flank
Left wide mid didn't track back after we lost the ball. Opposition has a 2v1 on that flank — one CB against two attackers, with no help.
