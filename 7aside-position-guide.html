2 · 3 · 1 System — In & Out of Possession

GK

Goalkeeper

Last line & first attacker
IN POSSESSION
  • DO Distribute quickly to wide areas
  • DO Act as sweeper — come off line for through balls
  • DO Give the CBs a short option when pressed
  • DON'T Hold the ball — play fast
  • DON'T Always play short — vary the outlet
OUT OF POSSESSION
  • DO Set your position early — don't wait
  • DO Communicate constantly with CBs
  • DO Narrow the angle on 1v1s
  • DON'T Come too far off your line unless certain
  • DON'T Go to ground too early

Key trait: Distribution is your superpower — you start every attack

CB

Centre Backs

Foundation of the system
IN POSSESSION
  • DO Stay available for the GK and keep our build-up shape
  • DO Use the DM as your primary reset option
  • DO Split wide to give width when GK has the ball
  • DON'T Both step forward at the same time
  • DON'T Force it — recycle if nothing is on
OUT OF POSSESSION
  • DO Hold your line — don't both chase
  • DO Communicate — one pushes, one covers
  • DO Stay goalside and don't be dragged wide
  • DON'T Follow the striker's run into midfield
  • DON'T Leave space in behind with a high line

Key trait: Composure under pressure — if you panic, the whole team panics

DM

Defensive Mid

The anchor — makes everything work
IN POSSESSION
  • DO Stay between the CBs and wide mids — always available
  • DO Receive, turn, and play forward quickly
  • DO Shift laterally to stay in the passing lane
  • DON'T Follow the wide mids forward
  • DON'T Drift wide — own the central corridor
OUT OF POSSESSION
  • DO Cover when a wide CM hasn't tracked back
  • DO Win second balls — be aggressive
  • DO Communicate — tell CBs when you're covering
  • DON'T Ball-watch — track runners through the middle
  • DON'T Commit to pressing and leave the centre empty

Key trait: The anchor that gives the wide mids their freedom — protect this space at all costs

WM

Wide Midfielders

Engine of attack & defensive discipline
IN POSSESSION
  • DO Push high and wide — stretch the opposition
  • DO Look to arrive late into the box
  • DO Read when it's safe to go — secure possession first
  • DON'T Both push forward at the same time
  • DON'T Stay glued to the touchline — cut inside too
OUT OF POSSESSION
  • DO Track back immediately on losing the ball
  • DO Get into your defensive position within 3 seconds
  • DO Press the wide CB if the ball goes there
  • DON'T Assume the DM will cover for you
  • DON'T Ball-watch — track the run on your side

Key trait: Your freedom in possession is earned by your work rate out of it

ST

Striker

Press trigger & finisher
IN POSSESSION
  • DO Hold up play — give wide mids time to arrive
  • DO Combine quickly with the attacking mid behind you
  • DO Make runs in behind to stretch the defence
  • DON'T Drop too deep — you'll crowd the midfield
  • DON'T Hold the ball too long in tight spaces
OUT OF POSSESSION
  • DO Press the first CB to trigger the team's shape
  • DO Force the opposition to play one way — show them outside
  • DO Be the signal — if you press, everyone presses
  • DON'T Sprint wildly — press with a purpose
  • DON'T Give up when the press is beaten — reset quickly

Key trait: You set the tempo for the whole team's defensive shape — your press starts everything

Glossary of Terms

AnchorA defensive midfielder who holds a fixed central position, protecting the defence and providing a reliable passing option.
Box-to-BoxA midfielder who contributes both defensively and offensively, covering the full length of the pitch.
Central CorridorThe vertical strip through the middle of the pitch. The most dangerous area to concede space in 7-a-side.
Closing DownPressing the player on the ball to reduce their time and options, forcing a mistake or backwards pass.
CompactnessKeeping the team's shape tight so there are no large gaps between players for the opposition to exploit.
CoverWhen a teammate positions themselves to support a player who is pressing or has been beaten, providing a second line of defence.
Defensive BlockA compact defensive shape where the team sits in organised lines, making it hard for the opposition to play through.
Defensive LineThe horizontal line your defenders hold. Pushing it high compresses space; dropping it deeper protects against balls in behind.
DistributionHow the goalkeeper plays the ball out — passes, throws, or kicks to restart an attack.
Hold-Up PlayWhen a striker receives with their back to goal and retains possession long enough for teammates to arrive in support.
Late RunA run into the box timed to arrive just as the ball is delivered — hard to track because it comes from deep.
OverloadCreating a numerical advantage in one area — e.g. two attackers against one defender on the flank.
Passing LaneThe open channel between players through which a pass can be played. Moving into a passing lane makes you available.
PivotA central player (usually the DM) through whom the team plays to switch direction or relieve pressure.
Press TriggerA signal — often the striker's first press — that tells the whole team to push up and press simultaneously.
RecyclePlaying the ball backwards or sideways when no forward option is on, to reset and try again from a better position.
ResetQuickly re-organising into the team's shape after the ball is lost or won.
ShapeThe team's formation and how players are positioned relative to each other. Maintaining shape means holding those positions.
Show OutsidePressing an opponent to steer them towards the touchline, away from the dangerous central areas.
Sweeper-KeeperA goalkeeper who comes off their line to act as an extra defender, intercepting through balls behind the defensive line.
Third-Man RunA run made by a third player during a 1-2 combination, arriving unexpectedly into space.
TransitionThe moment the ball changes hands. The most dangerous moment in 7-a-side — the split second after winning or losing possession.
